Register for My Account for individuals

Follow the steps below to register for access to your CRA account.

  1. Before you register

    If you register with a CRA user ID or an Interac sign-in partner, you can use the same credentials to access:

    • My Account
    • My Business Account
    • Represent a Client
    • Candidate Profile
    If you already have an account

    You do not need to register again. Sign in with your existing account credentials. You may be asked to provide the following information:

    • ???

  2. Gather your documents

    When you register for a CRA user ID or an Interac sign-in partner, you will need all of the following:

    • Your Social Insurance Number
      • Alternatives: a Temporary Tax Number (TTN) or Individual Tax Number (ITN)
    • Your date of birth
    • Your current postal code or ZIP code
    • Your tax returns for the 2 most recent tax years

    For faster access to all services, you will also need a mobile device and one of the following:

    • Your Canadian passport
    • Your Canadian driver's license
    • Your provincial photo ID card

  3. Choose how you want to sign in

    Choose which username and password process to use now to register, and in the future when you sign in.

    Not sure? Find the best options for your situation

    You can choose one of three ways to sign into your account. If you register for one and later decide you want to use a different sign-in option, you will need to register again.

    Interac sign-in partner

    Using an Interac sign-in partner is a good option if you are registered for online banking with one of our sign-in partner banks or credit unions and want to use the same username and password.

    CRA user ID

    CRA user ID is a good option if you prefer not to use your online banking information. You can create a separate username and password and use CRA sign in to log into your My Account.

    Provincial ID

    If you already have a BC Services Card or MyAlberta Digital ID, you can use your provincial ID to access most services in My Account. You cannot use a provincial partner with:

    • My Business Account or Represent a Client
    • Express Notice of Assessment
    • Auto-fill My Return

    You will not need to gather any documents prior to registration and you will not need to validate your identity if you choose to use your provincial ID to sign in. You will still need to enroll in multi-factor authentication the first time you sign in.

    GC Key

    You cannot use GC Key to sign into My Account.

    • Create a CRA user ID
      Help with CRA User ID

      How to choose memorable security questions.

      More help with CRA user ID

    • Register with an Interac® sign-in partner
      Help with Interac sign-in partners

      You must have an existing account with one of our Sign-in Partner banks or credit unions.

      Your banking credentials and tax information are never shared.

      On a shared device, do not use auto-complete for your banking sign in.

      More help with Interac sign-in partners

    • Use a provincial ID
      Help with provincial sign-in partners

      You must have an account with the province's website to register this way.

      Supported provincial partners:

      • BC Services Card
      • MyAlberta Digital ID

      You will not need to gather any documents prior to registration and you will not need to validate your identity if you choose to use your provincial ID to sign in. You will still need to enroll in multi-factor authentication the first time you sign in.

      You cannot use a provincial partner with:

      • My Business Account or Represent a Client
      • Express Notice of Assessment
      • Auto-fill My Return

      More help with provincial ID

  4. Validate your identity

    Choose an option to validate your identity:

    • Submit a photo of yourself and your ID document Option 1 of 2

      You can use the Interac document verification service to validate your identity immediately.

      Use a mobile device to take a real-time picture of yourself and an accepted identification document to validate your identity.

      You must be 16 years of age or older to use this service.

    • Wait for your CRA security code Option 2 of 2

      If you can't use the document verification service, a CRA security code will be sent to you within 7 to 14 days.

      Before the expiry date, sign in to My Account and follow the instructions to enter the CRA security code.

      Get a new CRA security code

      If your CRA security code expires, you will have to get a new security codeone.

    Get limited access before validating your identity

    You do not need to validate your identity to access the following services:

    • View status of your tax return
    • View RRSP deduction limit and TFSA contribution room
    • View notice of assessment or reassessment
    • Request a remittance voucher
    • Pay by pre-authorized debit (create new agreement)
    • Change notification preferences

  5. Enroll in multi-factor authentication (MFA)

    Choose one or both of these options to get your one-time passcode:

    • By phone Option 1 of 2

      You will need to provide at least one cell or landline number. You will be sent a one-time passcode that must be entered when you sign in to our online services.

    • With passcode grid Option 2 of 2

      The system will generate a unique passcode grid that you will need to save or print. You will use this grid every time you sign in to our online services.

    Help with one-time passcode

    Muti-factor authentication (MFA) increases the security of the CRA's online services by requiring users to enter a one-time passcode in addition to their password. MFA is mandatory for all users who wish to use the CRA sign-in services.

    If your account becomes locked due to entering the wrong passcode too many times, contact us to have your account unlocked.

    More help with multi-factor authentication

  6. Complete your identity validation

    Complete the validation option that you chose in step 3:

    • Interac document verification service Option 1 of 2

      Follow the instructions to complete this process on your mobile device.

    • CRA security code Option 2 of 2

      After you recieve your CRA security code in the mail, sign into My Account and enter your code before the CRA security code expiry date.

Your banking and tax information are never shared

  • The banking credentials you enter and the identity of the financial institution are not shared with the Government of Canada.

  • None of your personal tax information will be shared with your financial institution.

Choose memorable security questions

You will be asked to choosse 5 security questions and answers that will be used to recover your account if you forget your password.

You may want to choose questions that have more factual answers, instead of questions that can change over time or can have more than one answer.

For example, it may be easier to remember the make of your first car, instead of your favourite food or the name of a childhood friend that you may sometimes write as a nickname or with their last name.

Shared devices: do not use auto-complete for banking credentials

Make sure you do not use your browser's auto-complete for your online banking credentials if you register on a device you share with other people.

If you sign in with someone else's banking credentials by mistake and link your SIN number to their account, the other person will have access to your CRA My Account.

Accepted identification documents

The following original government-issued documents are accepted:

  • Your Canadian passport
  • Your Canadian driver's license
  • Your provincial photo ID card

We do not accept:

  • Your provincial health card
  • Documents that you have reported as lost or stolen
  • Expired or damaged documents
  • Pictures of photocopies

Tax returns

You will be asked to enter the amount you reported on:

  • line 15000
  • another variable line number

Use the numbers that you reported on the most recent tax return that you filed. Your tax return must be assessed and from either the current or previous tax year.

Use only the numbers that you reported on your return, even if your return was reassessed and the numbers have changed.

One-time passcode by phone

When you sign in, you can choose to receive your one-time passcode by:

  • text message
  • phone call from a toll-free number

If you did not receive your one-time passcode, you can:

  • ask for it to be resent or sent to a different telephone number that is already connected to your account
  • use the passcode grid if you are already enrolled with that option

If you can't receive your one-time passcode, please contact us.

More help with multi-factor authentication

One-time passcode by passcode grid

The passcode grid is a table made up of numbered rows and lettered columns, similar to a Bingo card.

We will ask for a specific combination of rows and columns to create your one-time passcode.

If you lose your passcode grid, you will still be able to sign in if you have added the telephone option for MFA. If you have not, you will need to contact us.
