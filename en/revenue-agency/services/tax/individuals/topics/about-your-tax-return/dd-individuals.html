Payments the CRA sends you

Direct deposit for individuals

With direct deposit, you receive your tax refunds and benefit payments faster and securely, directly in your account at a Canadian financial institution. In the event of an emergency or unforeseen circumstance, using direct deposit will ensure you continue to get your payments on time.

Sign up for direct deposit or update your information with your bank account information, which is on your personal cheques or online banking portal. If you can't find the information, contact your financial institution.

If you receive one of the following payments from the CRA and have a Canadian bank account, you can sign up for direct deposit:

  • Income tax refund
  • Goods and services tax/harmonized sales tax credit and any similar provincial and territorial payments
  • Canada child benefit and any similar provincial and territorial payments
  • Canada workers benefit
  • Deemed overpayment of tax
  • Canada Recovery Sickness Benefit, Canada Recovery Benefit, Canada Recovery Caregiving Benefit

Do not close your old bank account until your first payment has been deposited to your new bank account

If you can't access your account

If you do not have access to your account because you have a new phone number, you will have to update your information by phone.

For more help with your account, go to: Help guides for CRA sign-in accounts

Make a change for someone else

To change direct deposit information for someone else, including a spouse or family member, you must be their legal representative and use Represent a Client.

By phone

Updated immediately

Before you call
Have the following information ready to verify your identity:
  • Social Insurance Number (SIN)
  • Full name and date of birth
  • Complete address
  • Assessed tax return, notice of assessment or reassessment, or other tax document

If you have a CRA account, you can also sign into your account to access your tax information.

Make a change for someone else

You can only call on behalf of someone else in the following 2 situations:

  • The person is present on the call to verify their identity in English or French and provides verbal authorization for you to speak for them

  • You are the person's legal representative with full legal authority to act as them

    For details: Authorize a representative

By mail

Updated 4 to 6 weeks after we receive your documents

Change your address by mailing or faxing one of the following to your tax centre:

  • Completed Form RC325, Address Change Request

  • A letter which includes your:

    • Social insurance number (SIN)
    • New address
    • Date of your move
    • Signature
Make a change for someone else

To change the address of another individual, such as your spouse or common-law partner, they must sign a letter authorizing the change to their records. The letter must contain their:

  • Name
  • Social insurance number (SIN)
  • Signature
On your tax return

Updated when we assess your return, generally within 8 weeks of receiving it

You can update your personal information, including your address, by writing it on your paper tax return or using EFILE. You can not update it using NETFILE.

However, you should not wait for tax filing season to update your information. It is important that we have your most up-to-date information for many reasons:

  • We may need to contact you during the year for a variety of reasons (for example, to review programs, for collections activities, or security/identity theft issues)
  • We want to ensure that recurring payments and correspondence are being sent to the right place
  • In some cases, identification changes could have a direct impact on your eligibility for benefits (for example, change of province of residence or change of marital status)

Update your other personal information

You may also need to change your phone number, email address, or other details with the CRA. To change more of your personal information, go to: Update your personal information with the CRA

If you have moved

You may be eligible to claim some of your moving expenses. Learn more: Moving expenses

Change your address with other government departments

You need to contact each government organization that provides services to you to give them your new information. Go to: Change an address with a different department or agency

The CRA will not share your change of address address or other personal information, including direct deposit, with other government departments.
