Change your personal address

You may be looking for:

  • Change your business address
  • Change your trust address
  • Change your charity address
 

If you move, let the CRA know your new address as soon as possible.

Keeping your information up-to-date will ensure you receive all your payments and correspondences.

Online

Updated immediately

    CRA My Account

  3. Select Contact information
  4. Select Edit

If you can't access your account

If you do not have access to your account because you have a new phone number, you will have to update your information by phone.

For more help with your account, go to: Help guides for CRA sign-in accounts

Make a change for somone else

To change an address online for someone else, including a spouse or family member, you must be their legal representative and use Represent a Client.

By phone

Updated immediately

Before you call

Have your social insurance number (SIN) ready to verify your identity. Also, sign into your CRA account or gather the following information:

  • Full name and date of birth
  • Complete address
  • Assessed tax return, notice of assessment or reassessment, other tax document
Make a change for someone else

You can only call on behalf of someone else in the following 2 situations:

  • The person is present on the call to verify their identity in English or French and provides verbal authorization for you to speak for them

  • You are the person's legal representative with full legal authority to act as them

    For details: Authorize a representative

By mail

Updates will be made 4 to 6 weeks after we receive your documents by mail. For an immediate change, make your updates online or by phone.

Change your address by mailing or faxing one of the following to your tax centre:

  • Completed Form RC325, Address Change Request

  • A letter which includes your:

    • Social insurance number (SIN)
    • New address
    • Date of your move
    • Signature

Make a change for someone else

To change the address of another individual, such as your spouse or common-law partner, they must sign a letter authorizing the change to their records. The letter must contain their:

  • Name
  • Social insurance number (SIN)
  • Signature
On your tax return

Updates will be made when we assess your return, generally within 8 weeks of receiving it. For an immediate change, make your updates online or by phone.

You can update your personal information, including your address, by writing it on your paper tax return. You can not update it using EFILE or NETFILE.

However, you should not wait for tax filing season to update your information. It is important that we have your most up-to-date information for many reasons:

  • We may need to contact you during the year for a variety of reasons (for example, to review programs, for collections activities, or security/identity theft issues)
  • We want to ensure that recurring payments and correspondence are being sent to the right place
  • In some cases, identification changes could have a direct impact on your eligibility for benefits (for example, change of province of residence or change of marital status)

Because using a paper return to update information will take longer, it is better to update your information using one of the other methods.

Update your other personal information

You may also need to change your phone number, email address, or other details with the CRA. To update more of your personal information, go to: Update your information with the CRA

If you have moved

You may be eligible to claim some of your moving expenses. Learn more: Moving expenses

Change your address with other government departments

You need to contact each government organization that provides services to you to give them your new information. Go to: Change an address with a different department or agency

The CRA will not share your change of address address or other personal information, including direct deposit, with other government departments.
